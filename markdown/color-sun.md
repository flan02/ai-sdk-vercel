# What Color is the Sun?

The answer depends on whether you are looking at the sun from space or from Earth.

### 1. From Space: **White**
If you were to stand in the International Space Station and look at the sun, it would appear **pure white**.

Even though we often draw the sun as yellow, it actually emits light across all visible wavelengths of the color spectrum. When all these colors are combined, the human eye perceives them as white.

### 2. From Earth: **Yellow, Orange, or Red**
When viewed through Earth's atmosphere, the sun appears to change color due to a process called **Rayleigh scattering**:

*   **Midday:** The atmosphere scatters shorter wavelengths (blue and violet) more easily. This leaves the longer wavelengths (yellow/white) to reach your eyes, making the sun appear **yellowish-white**.
*   **Sunrise and Sunset:** When the sun is low on the horizon, the light has to travel through much more of the Earth's atmosphere to reach you. By the time the light arrives, almost all the blue and violet light has been scattered away, leaving only the longest wavelengths: **oranges and reds**.

### Summary Table
| Perspective | Perceived Color | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Outer Space** | White | All visible wavelengths are emitted equally. |
| **Earth (Noon)** | Yellowish-White | Atmosphere scatters blue light away. |
| **Earth (Sunset)** | Orange / Red | Atmosphere scatters almost all colors except long wavelengths. |
